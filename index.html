Recycling Matters

Why Recycle and How to Do It Right

Recycling is one of the most direct actions a household or business can take to reduce its environmental impact. Here is what you need to know.

Protects Our Environment

Recycling reduces the volume of waste sent to landfill, where decomposing materials release methane which is a greenhouse gas far more potent than carbon dioxide. By recycling, we actively reduce our contribution to climate change.

Conserves Natural Resources

Recycling reduces the need to extract and process raw materials, saving both energy and valuable resources. For instance, recycling just one tonne of paper can preserve around 17 trees and conserve up to 26,000 litres of water thereby protecting forests and ensuring cleaner water supplies for future generations.

Creates Local Employment

The recycling industry supports a broad range of jobs, from collection crews and sorters to facility operators and logistics personnel. Every tonne recycled in Estcourt contributes to local economic activity and job creation.

Reduces Water and Air Pollution

When waste ends up in landfills, toxic leachate can seep into groundwater, while incineration releases harmful gases into the atmosphere. Recycling helps prevent both problems, protecting our water sources and improving the air quality we all depend on. By choosing to recycle, we actively contribute to healthier communities and a cleaner environment.

Paper and Cardboard

  • Flatten all cardboard boxes before placing them in the recycling bin.
  • Remove any plastic liners, tape, or staples where possible.
  • Keep paper and cardboard dry. Wet paper is not recyclable.
  • Do not include wax-coated or food-contaminated cardboard.

Plastics

  • Rinse containers thoroughly to remove food residue before recycling.
  • Check for the recycling symbol. Plastics numbered 1 (PET) and 2 (HDPE) are most widely accepted.
  • Remove lids where possible, as they are often a different plastic type.
  • Do not include plastic bags or cling wrap in standard recycling bins.

Metals

  • Rinse all cans and tins to remove food residue.
  • Both aluminium and steel cans are recyclable. Aluminium is particularly valuable.
  • Crush cans to save space, but ensure they remain identifiable as metal.
  • Remove labels where possible, although this is not strictly required.

Glass

  • Rinse all glass bottles and jars before recycling.
  • Do not include broken glass, mirrors, window glass, or ceramics in standard recycling.
  • Remove metal lids and recycle them separately as metal.
